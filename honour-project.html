Mean-Variance Hedging With Neural Networks Using Heston Model

Honours Project 2024

Abstract

We investigate the effectiveness of hedging a European basket options using the Heston Model within a contractual framework involving two parties. The contractual agreement stipulatesthat at a time T, if the value of the stock owned by party one exceeds that of party two, a trade occurs. We make use of the Heston Model to optimise the hedging strategy, considering factors such as volatility of the respective stocks, co-variation of both stocks with their respective variances and co-variation between the two stocks.

Specifically, we explore how covariance between the two underlying stocks, as well as their respective covariances with volatilities influence option pricing and hedging outcomes. To estimate the parameters of the Heston Model, our first step is to discretise the model utilising Eulerâ€™s discretisation method. Thereafter, employ Maximum Likelihood Estimation on the joint multivariate normal distribution of the two stocks to estimate the parameters of the model.
